Everything you need to know about studying abroad with Hines

For UK Foundation programmes, the minimum entry requirements are:

  • 5 WAEC credits including English Language and Mathematics (C6 or above)
  • Some universities accept D7 with additional supporting qualifications
  • IELTS: 5.5 minimum, or an equivalent English proficiency test
We can often find foundation programmes even when you are missing one requirement — chat with an advisor to explore your options.

Yes — it depends on the university and the strength of your overall profile. Options include:

  • Some UK universities accept a Third Class with 2–3 years of relevant professional experience
  • US universities may consider strong GRE or GMAT scores alongside a lower GPA
  • Canada generally requires a 2:2 or higher, though pre-Masters bridging programmes exist
  • We have partner universities that specifically cater to non-standard academic profiles
We have successfully placed many Third Class students into postgraduate programmes. Chat with an advisor to see what is possible for your specific situation.

Our model is fully transparent. Here is exactly how it works:

  • Free: Initial consultation, eligibility assessment, university matching, and application submission to partner institutions
  • Free: We earn a commission from partner universities — not from students
  • Paid (optional): Visa processing support, IELTS preparation, and scholarship essay editing are available as additional services
Basic placement from assessment to offer letter is completely free for students. You only pay the university's own application fee directly.

Timelines vary by destination and intake, but typical durations are:

  • UK: 4–8 weeks from submission to conditional offer; 10–16 weeks for visa processing
  • US: 3–6 months including test prep, essays, and financial aid applications
  • Canada: 6–10 weeks for offer letter; 8–12 weeks for study permit
We recommend starting at least 6 months before your intended intake date to avoid rushing any stage of the process.
